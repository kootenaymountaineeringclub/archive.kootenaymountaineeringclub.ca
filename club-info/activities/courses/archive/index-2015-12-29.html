Canadian Red Cross Wilderness and Remote First Aid

November 6, 7 & 8, 2015

In isolated areas or wilderness settings, where equipment, communication and assistance is limited, adventurers need a higher level of training to work and respond to emergencies. This course teaches comprehensive first aid and CPR techniques for hikers, bikers, climbers, skiers, boaters, hunters, fishers, and anyone who travels off the pavement.

The course includes the material in Standard First Aid & CPR‐C, plus strategies for providing extended care for up to 24 hours. Topics covered include airway, breathing and circulation emergencies, and secondary surveys to determine any other life threatening or ‘trip‐threatening’ conditions. Also included is theory and practical skills to increase proficiency in small wound care, head and spinal injuries, muscle, bone and joint injuries. Sudden medical emergencies, poisonings, and environmental hazards will also be covered.

A significant component of the course is taught in an outdoor setting and requires greater physical activity and endurance than typical first aid training. Though we will not be backpacking or staying out overnight, please come prepared to spend time outside, whatever the weather.

  • No pre-requisites are required
  • 20‐hours of high quality instruction
  • A high quality water‐proof, tear resistant, packable wilderness medicine book
  • Successful completion results in a 3 year nationally recognized certification

For more information, or to register please contact Darcey Lutz at D2Lutz@telus.net The course is taught at the Passmore Fire Hall in the Slocan Valley - see http://www.rdck.ca/assets/Services/Recreation/Chelseys~Graphics/Web_Slocan_Fall15_Final.pdf

Introduction to Digital Navigation

  • Date/Time: Saturday, November 21, 8:30 AM – 12:30 PM
  • Repeat course: January 9, 2016
  • Place: Castlegar Community Complex PURCELL room - 2101 6th Ave, Castlegar, BC
  • Instructor: Judson Wright, Ski Guide (ACMG)
  • Cafeteria: will be open
  • Cost: $20 for members (cash) - $35 (cash) for non-members, to be paid at the start of the course.
  • Registration: email peteroostlander@hotmail.com to reserve your spot

This course is designed to introduce members to GPS and its use in backcountry navigation. Note: The instructor will use the most common brand of GPS used in this area: GARMIN. If you own a GPS (any brand), please bring it to class.

By the end of the session, participants will:

  • Understand how the Global Positioning System functions.
  • Understand basic navigation terminology
  • Understand features and functions common to all GPS standalone devices.
  • Learn how to apply these features and functions in a wilderness navigation context.
  • Be familiar with other navigation resources available
  • Learn how to create a route
  • Learn to export data to a format that can be emailed from Google earth or from the GPS
  • Understand how to transfer and view tracks in Google earth and back to the GPS
  • Understand Google earth navigation features
  • Understand the main differences (drawbacks and usefulness) between smart phones and GPS units

This is a great follow-up to the past April 2015 KMC course Intro to Wilderness Navigation - Map and Compass Use, although it is not a prerequisite.

The introductory course, Avalanche Skills Training 1, is a 2 day weekend program. Students will learn how to recognize and manage avalanche terrain, read snow pack information and better choose up track routes and descent runs.  The program is lecture based the first day, with some time dedicated in the field to practice transceiver searches and discuss rescue fundamentals. The second day will be field based and experiential, learning and practicing skills like: terrain evaluation, mountain hazard assessment, safe route finding techniques, snow pack analysis and snow testing techniques. Please note though the course is introductory in nature to backcountry skiing, you need to have intermediate to advanced resort skiing or boarding ability.
